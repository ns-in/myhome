About / Description

Saurkundi Pass/ Rani Sue Lake a Beautiful trek in the Kullu valley for those who are looking for a weekend getaway. The trek takes you to the high altitude lake of Rani Sui on the way to Kalihani Pass and Bara Bhangal. You also get some fabulous views of Manalsu glacier, Dhauladhar and Pirpanjal ranges including Hanuman Tibba, Deo Tibba and Rohtang Pass.

On this visit, you will witness a sacred lake Saurkundi that is accepted to be committed to Maharishi Shandilya. Individuals have a conviction that it is sacrosanct to take a plunge in the water of this lake. Saurkundi pass trek offers a life-changing 360-degree perspective of the prototypical nature, untamed by people to such an extent. With an elevation of 13000 feet the Saurkund, the beautiful lake of the Saurkundi pass will bring surely a lot of positiveness and fill your mind with the pure Himalayan beauty.

The magic of Saurkundi pass trek is that you can experience ever changing the landscape of Himalaya rapidly. Saurkundi pass trek is the best trekking expedition in Himachal to experience the culture and environment of Himalayan villages, local lifestyle and magnificent views.

The trekking trip to Saurkundi pass can be more thrilling and exciting depending on the duration of your trip. There are a lot of interesting places around Saurkundi pass that you can explore varying with your interest.

Short Itinerary

Day 1 : Manali >> Lama Dug (6 to 7 hours)
Day 2 : Lama Dug >> Khanpari>> Rani Sui lake ( 5 to 6 hours)
Day 3 : Rani Sui Lake>> Khaniyal >> Manali

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1 : Manali >> Lama Dug
We start the trek from Dhungri about 1.5kms from Manali, going past the famous Hadimba temple. The trail climbs steeply to thick forests of Deodar and Pine. There is no water point after departing from Manali until we reach Lama Dugh - five hours away. As one gains height, the distinct views of the different faces of Indrasan, Deo Tibba and the peaks of Bara Shigri glacier open up. Multiple vantage points offer panoramic views of Manali and the area above it. In the last leg, the path turns to the right and one traverses the ridge horizontally and enters the campsite, an alpine plateau studded with the thick growth of alpine flowers.

Day 2 : Lama Dug >> Khanpari>> Rani Sui lake
The height gain on this day is approximately 1000m. The trek is through a steep ridge below Khanpari Tibba (4000m). From this point, one can have a magnificent glimpse of Manali, situated on the left bank of River Beas and the whole upper Kullu valley, which appears swathed in the different colours of the season. Rhodo and Birch shrubs pave the way to the top. After reaching the ridge, Manali lies to our east and Kullu to the west. We cut through the ridges westward to reach the lake at Rani Sui, a small mountain tarn with a 360 degree view of the Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges. We camp near the lake for the night, as we witness a starry canvas enfold above us and breathe in the crisp mountain air.

Day 3 : Rani Sui>> Khaniyal >> Manali
It’s a steady, easy descent today through high altitude meadows – swathed in wild flowers and pine, maple, horse chestnut and walnut forests to reach Duddu Thach. We continue onward through dense pine forests to reach Khaniyal. A vehicle will meet us there and drop us to Manali, concluding the trek.
